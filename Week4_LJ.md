## Content Insights:
This week I learned about deployment. 
During development of a site, there are three different stages of the site. 
First is the local development environment, which is hosted locally by a combination of localhost software and a virtual machine on the developer's computer. Once edits are made to the site's files, the developer pushes them to a staging environment, which is a private site, typically password protected. The staging environment is as close to the real site as possible and is used to test the site in real time. Finally, after testing, the changes are pushed to the production site, which is the official, publicly accessible website.
